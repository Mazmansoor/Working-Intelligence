Module 1: What Programming Really Is

The Goal

Before we write code, we must understand the mindset. You are about to learn a new way of thinking.

1. Computers are Literal, Not Smart

A computer is a machine that follows instructions. It has no intuition. It has no common sense.

If you tell a human, "Please go to the store," they know you imply "Walk out the door, go to the street, avoid cars, enter the shop."

If you tell a robot "Go to the store," it might walk through the wall because you didn't tell it to open the door first.

Programming is the act of being precise.

2. Precision vs. Intention

  • Intention: What you want to happen. ("Make a sandwich")
  • Code: The exact steps to make it happen. ("Pick up bread. Pick up knife. Open jar...")

When you start, your code will often fail. This is usually because your intention was clear, but your instructions were incomplete.

3. Errors are Friends, Not Enemies

Beginners fear red text. They see an error message and think, "I broke it" or "I am bad at this."

This is wrong.

An error message is the computer trying to help you. It is saying, "I want to do what you asked, but I don't understand this specific part."

  • Read the error.
  • Don't panic.
  • Fix the instruction.

4. Activity: The Robot Sandwich

Note: This is a thought experiment.

Write down the steps to make a peanut butter and jelly sandwich.

Now, imagine a robot following them literally.

  • Did you say to take the lid off the jar?
  • Did you say to use a knife?
  • Did you say to put the bread pieces together?

If you missed a step, the robot fails. Programming is simply practicing this level of detail.
